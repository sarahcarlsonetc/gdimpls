Who Am I?

Hello, there. I'm Sarah.

I'm a bibliophile, early-riser, and twin sister. I graduated from the University of Minnesota with a degree in English and minor concentration in Mass Communication. And what in the world do you do with that?

I've interned as an investigative journalist for a start-up online public affairs publication and in non-profit literary publishing at Coffee House Press. I've worked retail, babysat, mentored first-year students in the College of Liberal Arts at UMN, and as a student assistant archivist in the Givens Collection of African American Literature and the University of Minnesota Libraries. I volunteered as a poetry editor for a student-run online lit mag with low readership, and served as Editor in Cheif for the 2015 edition of Ivory Tower, the undergraduate literary arts magazine at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities.

Now, I'm the Project Manager for Umbra: Search African American History (umbrasearch.org), a grant-funded project coming out the Givens Collection of African American Literature.

So, I did use my Liberal Arts degree in a meaningful way! I engage with total strangers at institutions from across the country every day to get things done. Words, baby. Just words.
